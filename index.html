MANIFESTO

Common Sense for the Common People.

  1. Real freedom is the absence of authority.
  2. Everyone should have the right to live and organise how they please, provided they do not exploit others.
  3. Communities should make their own decisions through voluntary agreement, where power stays at the local level.
  4. Society should work through meaningful co-operation, where people can choose how they participate.
  5. No one is compelled to labour on projects irrelevant to them.
  6. A communal economy, where trade is free and co-operative, is the only fair system.
  7. We reject private ownership of tools, spaces, and resources. They should belong to the community as a whole.
  8. Welfare is built through mutual aid, so the means of it should remain in the hands of those who need it.
  9. Freedom of expression should be defended to encourage a diversity of ideas.
